# Insights from Student Performance Analysis

## 1. Bar Chart (Gender vs Average Score)
The bar chart shows that female students have a higher average score (around 72.93) compared to male students (around 64.65).  
This indicates a noticeable performance gap where female students are outperforming male students in this dataset.

## 2. Pie Chart (Gender-wise Average Contribution)
The pie chart highlights the proportion of average scores by gender.  
It shows that female students contribute a larger share to overall performance compared to male students.

## 3. Scatter Plot (Attendance vs Average Score)
The scatter plot shows a clear positive trend between attendance and average score.  
Students with higher attendance tend to score higher, while those with lower attendance generally have lower scores.

## 4. Correlation Analysis (Study Hours vs Average Score)
The calculated correlation value is 0.888, indicating a strong positive relationship.  
This means that students who study more hours tend to achieve significantly better results.

## 5. Bar Chart (Top 10 Students)
From the top 10 students data, it is observed that high-performing students consistently have high study hours (5–7 hours) and high attendance (above 90%).  
This suggests that both consistency and effort are key factors for top performance.

## 6. Histogram (Distribution of Average Scores)
The histogram shows that most students fall within the 60–80 score range.  
Very few students are in the extremely low or high ranges, indicating that the majority of students perform at an average level.

## 7. Histogram (Pass vs Fail Distribution)
The histogram shows that most students are in the pass category, while only a small number fall into the fail category.  
This indicates that overall class performance is fairly good.

## 8. Grade Classification (A, B, C, D)
The grade distribution shows that most students fall into B and C categories, indicating moderate performance.  
A smaller number of students achieve A grades, while a few fall into the D category.

## 9. Overall Performance Summary
The overall class average is approximately 69.07, which suggests that the class is performing above average but not at an excellent level.

## Final Key Insight
Study hours and attendance are the most important factors influencing student performance.  
Students who consistently attend classes and spend more time studying tend to achieve higher scores.
